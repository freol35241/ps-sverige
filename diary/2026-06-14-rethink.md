# 2026-06-14 — Rethink: shelving the voter tool, manifesto data, engineer's framing

User session ended with a major pivot. Recording the new direction here so
the article work has a single reference point.

## Decisions made

1. **Voter tool shelved.** The blind-argument-excerpt design didn't work
   (statements of fact rather than stances). The three-round redesign
   also stopped feeling right. The whole tool is removed from public
   navigation. The `/valkompassen` route remains as a file but is not
   linked. The matching backend and curated distillations remain in
   place as potential future material.
2. **Site language switched to English.** All four pages, the figure
   labels, the methodology — everything user-facing. Some internal
   variables and analysis-code comments stay as-is.
3. **New analytical frame: politics as a control loop.** Four elements:
   - **Diagnosis** — what is the problem? (sensor reading)
   - **End state** — what should the result look like? (setpoint)
   - **Mechanism** — how do we get from A to B? (controller)
   - **Values** — the upstream layer that shapes all three
4. **New data source: party manifestos.** Compare stated positions
   (manifesto text) with revealed positions (Riksdag votes and
   reservations). The gap between them is the publishable headline.

## Probe results: where to get manifesto data

Two viable sources, in order of preference:

### Vi vill (SND/Swedish National Data Service) — recommended

- URL: <https://snd.se/en/catalogue/dataset/snd0810-1/3>
- Bulk zip: <https://api.researchdata.se/dataset/snd0810-1/3/file/data?filePath=Svenska%20partiprogram%20och%20valmanifest.zip>
- ~242 MB zip, PDF + TXT of all Swedish party programs and election
  manifestos from 1897 onwards
- Metadata in `Partidokument.csv` (title, source, type)
- Free, no registration, CC public domain
- Includes 2024 EU election manifestos, almost certainly 2022 Riksdag
  election manifestos as well
- Status note: collection marked "deprecated 2022-09" but documents
  added through 2024 are visible

This is the cleanest option: we get raw text, do our own NLP, and don't
inherit anyone else's coding scheme.

### MARPOR / Manifesto Project — complementary

- URL: <https://manifesto-project.wzb.eu/datasets>
- Coded manifesto data using a standardised political-science taxonomy
  (per101 to per706: free enterprise, welfare state expansion, etc.)
- Covers Swedish elections from 1944 onwards
- Confirmed to include 2022 election in the MPDS2022a release
- Available in Stata, SPSS, CSV, XLSX — and via the `manifestoR` package
- Free, but requires registration for API access
- Trade-off: standardised but academic; saves us doing our own coding
  but constrains us to their categories

We'll use Vi vill for raw text. MARPOR is interesting as a sanity check
if our category mappings line up with theirs, but not strictly needed.

## Proposed article structure (engineer's view of politics)

### Intro (~250 words)

Hook: most political analysis stops at "what parties say they want."
Engineering view: politics is a control loop. Diagnose the problem,
define a target state, design a mechanism. Disagreement can occur on
all three — and the upstream layer of values colours all of them.
What if we had data on all three for each party?

### Part 1 — Diagnosis: what do parties think is wrong?

Source: opening pages of each manifesto, where parties typically
declare "Sweden faces a crisis of …". NLP extraction of the named
problem entities. Comparison across parties.

Example expected finding: SD diagnoses migration and identity; S
diagnoses inequality and welfare cuts; M diagnoses crime and
permissiveness; V diagnoses inequality and class power; MP diagnoses
climate.

### Part 2 — End state: what do parties want Sweden to be?

Source: aspirational closing chapters of manifestos. NLP extraction
of named end-states. Comparison.

Example finding: end states differ less than diagnoses. Almost all
parties claim to want a fairer, safer, more prosperous Sweden — they
differ on emphasis, not on label.

### Part 3 — Mechanism: how do parties propose to get there?

Source: policy proposals in manifestos, cross-referenced with
revealed positions (Riksdag voting record, reservation co-signing,
HOW lexicon already built).

This is where the existing analysis (HOW axis, vote stance, three-axis
gap) plugs in directly. The Tidö-collapse finding fits here: revealed
mechanism converging despite stated mechanism diverging.

### Part 4 — Values: the upstream layer

Source: invoked values in manifestos and reservations. Frequency
analysis of value-language ("frihet", "rättvisa", "ansvar", "trygghet",
"solidaritet", etc.). Mapping value vocabulary to the four upstream
axes.

The values-language analysis is where the WHY-axis work from earlier
plugs in. The point: values don't change the mechanism directly —
they change which diagnoses you accept, which end states you want, and
which mechanisms you find tolerable.

### Part 5 — The gap: stated vs revealed

The headline section. For each party, compare their manifesto's
stated mechanism (e.g., L 2022: "stark rättssäkerhet") with their
revealed mechanism (votes on hemliga tvångsmedel, surveillance
expansions inside the Tidö agreement). The gap is the story.

Existing findings fit here:
- SD reservation collapse: revealed behaviour diverging from stated
  oppositional posture
- M ↔ SD rhetorical bleed: revealed convergence of register that
  manifestos may not yet show
- S ↔ C marriage-of-convenience: their manifestos probably read more
  similarly than their reservations do
