# 2026-06-13

First session. Bootstrapped the Riksdag behavioural pipeline end-to-end:
fetch → matrix → embed → map → defector index.

## Data pipeline

The bulk dumps under `data.riksdagen.se/dataset/votering/votering-<rm>.json.zip`
are the clean path: 4 zips × ~10 MB cover the full current mandate (riksmöten
2022/23–2025/26). No pagination, no API key. Each entry inside a zip is one
vote event with one row per MP (349 rows). The IDEA's pagination-free claim is
correct.

Schema confirmed: stable `intressent_id` per MP, unique `votering_id` per vote
event, `rost ∈ {Ja, Nej, Avstår, Frånvarande}`, plus `parti` recorded
at-time-of-vote (matters for politisk vilde tracking).

## Sanity numbers

- 872,849 vote rows = 2,501 vote events × 349 nominal seats. ✓
- 424 unique MPs across the mandate (turnover via substitutes).
- Modal-party seat shares: S 126, SD 86, M 81, C 29, V 28, MP 27, L 22, KD 21,
  − 2. Ratios match the 2022 election. ✓
- Vote response mix: Ja 59.5 %, Nej 17.5 %, Frånvarande 14.0 %, Avstår 8.9 %.
- All votes in the dumps are `votering=huvud` (main, not preliminary). No
  leakage of test votes.

## Encoding decision

For the matrix: Ja=+1, Nej=−1, Avstår=0, Frånvarande=NaN. Avstår is *present
and abstaining* and is itself a discipline signal — coding it as the neutral
midpoint preserves that. Frånvarande is missing data and gets column-mean
imputed at PCA time.

Filter for the fit set: `n_present_votes ≥ 200`. 404 of 424 MPs make it; the
20 excluded are short-serving substitutes whose coordinates would be noisy.
Their PCs are still computed by projection for completeness.

## Embedding

PCA on the (404 × 2,501) imputed-centred matrix.

| PC | Variance | Interpretation                                  |
|----|---------:|-------------------------------------------------|
| 1  |   46.0 % | Government / opposition (Tidö vs S/V/MP/C)      |

| 3  |    9.9 % | (Tied to L↔KD discrimination; see Avci below)   |

Two PCs already explain 60 % of all vote variance. The "horseshoe" / Y-shape
on PC1–PC2 is the classic roll-call pattern (Poole–Rosenthal). The opposition

Party centroids on PC1 land in order V (-10.2) < MP (-9.0) < − (-7.7) <
C (-0.2) < L (+14.6) < M (+17.8) < KD (+18.7) < SD (+23.4), with S apart at
(-27.8, +8.2). C sits at PC1 ≈ 0 but with strongly negative PC2 — centrist
on the bloc axis but voting with the V/MP arm.

## Defector index

Per MP, in the full 4-D PCA space:

```
d_own  = ‖x − centroid(own party)‖
d_near = min over other parties ‖x − centroid(p)‖
ratio  = d_own / d_near    > 1 ⇒ behaviourally inside another party
```

### Findings worth keeping

- **Liberalerna is the unstable party of the mandate.** 8 of the top 12
  defectors are L. The party splits behaviourally: 11 of 21 L MPs sit
  closest to M, 9 to KD. Only Larry-edge cases stay nearest to their own
  centroid in the full crosstab.
- **Gulan Avci sits 0.12 units from the KD centroid vs 4.95 from L's** —
  her 4-D coordinates `(18.7, 2.6, −13.4, 0.9)` are identical to KD's
  `(18.7, 2.5, −13.4, 1.0)` to two decimals. The Liberal Party leader
  votes like a Christian Democrat. Worth following up: is it a leadership
  signalling effect, or did the L group genuinely realign during the
  mandate?
- **Politisk vilde validation.** Elsa Widding (former SD) classifies as
  nearest-SD; Jamal El-Haj (former S) classifies as nearest-S. Both are
  publicly known cases; the method reproduces them without supervision,
  which is reassuring.
- **Crosstab of nearest-other party** is a clean structural map of the
  parliament:
  - C → MP (24 of 27) — C votes with the soft left.
  - SD → L (71 of 81) — Tidö coupling shows up as SD's nearest neighbour
    being the leftmost gov party, not its ideological cousin KD.
  - MP ↔ V (17 ↔ 26) — symmetric pairing.

### Party cohesion ranking

Mean within-party distance to centroid (smaller = more disciplined):

```
KD 2.51   M 5.20   L 6.57   C 6.63   SD 6.69   S 7.30   V 7.52   MP 8.48
```

KD is dramatically tighter than anyone else. MP is the loosest. The
government parties (except L) are more cohesive than the opposition. Note:
this metric mixes "discipline" and "ideological narrowness" and shouldn't
be over-interpreted without controls.

## Caveats / known issues

- **Imputation choice.** Column-mean imputation biases absent MPs toward
  the parliament-average position. Alternatives: pairwise-agreement
  cosine over commonly voted items, or low-rank EM (Poole-Rosenthal use
  the latter). Worth re-running with cosine-similarity to compare.
- **`avser=motivfrågan` votes** (1.5 % of rows) are included; they vote
  on the motivation/reasoning rather than the substantive question. Tiny
  share, but a robustness check filtering to `sakfrågan` only is on the
  list.
- **Substitutes** with 100 ≤ n < 200 votes are projected but not in the
  fit. The cutoff is arbitrary; sensitivity to it untested.
- **The Tidö cluster is overplotted on the headline figure** — fine for
  the macro story, but obscures the within-bloc structure that PC3/PC4
  probably contain. A per-bloc zoom is a next step.

## Phase 1 — temporal drift

### Setup

`src/temporal.py` fits PCA on each riksmöte independently with
`MIN_VOTES_PER_YEAR = 80`, then Procrustes-aligns consecutive years on the
overlap set (MPs in fit in both years). I do scale-match the overlap RMS
after rotation; without that, year-t and year-(t-1) have arbitrary PC
magnitudes and the trajectories are uninterpretable. The price is that a
uniform chamber-wide drift in any direction becomes invisible — only
differential per-party movement shows up. Acceptable for this analysis.

### Trajectory figure caveat → real finding

The aligned trajectories showed the entire Tidö bloc (M, KD, L, SD)
collapsing onto each other in 2025/26: mean pairwise centroid distance
9.08 → 7.96 → 8.24 → **0.43**. I was worried this was a Procrustes-rescale
artefact, so I did a model-free check: share of votes where all four
parties cast the same majority.

```
            M+KD+L+SD     M+KD+L (cabinet)   S+V+MP+C (opp)
2022/23     67%           100%               9%
2023/24     73%           100%               12%
2024/25     73%           100%               10%
2025/26     98%           100%               10%
```

The collapse is real. But the framing has to be sharpened: the cabinet
M+KD+L has voted as a unit (100%) since day one — that's structural,
cabinet members vote together by definition. **The convergence is
entirely driven by SD aligning with the cabinet**: SD↔M pairwise agreement
went 67% → 73% → 73% → 98%. By the election year, SD is a de facto fourth
cabinet party in the chamber. The original IDEA framing was right that
strategic abstention and bloc structure carry signal — what's surprising is
how recently and abruptly the SD↔cabinet bond tightened.

This also kills the "L drifted toward KD" hypothesis from yesterday's
diary. **L↔KD pairwise agreement has been 100% since 2022/23.** The
Gulan-Avci-sits-on-KD-centroid finding wasn't drift, it was the trivial
consequence of cabinet membership — *every* L MP sits near KD because L
MPs *are* KD voters, in the chamber sense. The personality framing
("Liberal leader votes like a Christian Democrat") was misleading. The
correct framing is that the cabinet votes as one in the chamber. Within-L
heterogeneity is the more interesting story and is what PC2 picked up.

### Opposition contrast

The opposition (S+V+MP+C) never coheres — four-way agreement stays at
~10% throughout. They are not a coalition. PC2 also separates V/MP/C
from S, which matches: only V+MP coordinate. C votes with the soft-left
on ~50% of votes (L↔C 49% in 2025/26) but doesn't lock in.

### Speech-vs-vote cross-validation

Three approaches tried; the first and third are the keepers.

**1. TF-IDF word 1-2grams + Truncated SVD + 2D PCA, Procrustes-aligned to
the vote-PC space.** PC1 correlation = 0.43, PC2 corr = 0.42. Meaningful
but visually noisy — at the MP level the speech embedding is a fuzzy
cloud.

**2. Joint word + character 3-5gram TF-IDF.** PC1 corr dropped to 0.19.
Character n-grams added more noise than signal. The chamber's institutional
register is strong: function-word frequency is similar across parties.
Rejected.

**3. Direct test — predict party from MP speeches.** Logistic regression on
TF-IDF features, 5-fold CV: **74.8% accuracy** vs 28.6% modal-class
baseline. The MP→party signal is strong; it just doesn't compress cleanly
to 2D. Per-party recalls:

```
S 96%   SD 86%   V 83%   KD 71%   MP 67%   M 56%   C 33%   L 33%
```

Distinct cleanly: S, SD, V. Conflated: L, C. Reads the cabinet voice:
L→KD confusion 67%, M→SD confusion 28%.

### Notable cross-bloc confusion: M ↔ SD

Moderaterna's speech text gets predicted as SD 28% of the time. This is
a different signal from the Tidö-convergence vote finding: vote
convergence happened in the SD direction (SD adopted cabinet votes), but
rhetoric is moving the other way — M is using vocabulary that resembles
SD. The two are independent and both run-up to election year. Worth
flagging for a temporal-stylistic follow-up.

## Decisions, caveats

- **Cabinet ministers speak as the government.** I haven't separated
  ministerial speeches from back-bench MP speeches. The L/KD/M confusion
  cluster in the classifier confusion matrix is partly this — ministers'
  speeches are written by ministry staff. A real per-party stylometry
  needs to filter to back-bench speeches.
- **Filter parameters set by trial.** `MIN_WORDS = 10_000` and
  `MIN_SPEECHES = 30` for the stylometry. Sensitivity not tested.
- **2D visualisation of TF-IDF is misleading.** The dimensionality is too
  high for a 2D projection to carry the signal. The classifier accuracy
  is the better summary.
- **The speech corpus is now downloaded and parsed (66 MB parquet) so
  the LLM-authorship study has its baseline ready** — pre-2023 isn't in
  the current mandate, but the 2022/23 riksmöte runs Sept 2022 → June
  2023, mostly pre-ChatGPT-popular-use. The 2022/23 dump is the asset.

# 2026-06-13 (session 3)

Built phase 3 v0 — the revealed-preference compass — plus a three-axis
extension (what / why / how) that came out of a design conversation about
why standard valkompasses are condescending.

## The design conversation, captured

A normal valkompass asks "what" (do you support policy X) but ignores "why"
(values) and "how" (theory of change). Two voters giving the same answer
for opposite reasons get matched to the same party — and one of them is
going to be miserable.

The pivot: **the "why" is already in the corpus, ignored**. Every
consequential vote event has reservationer (written party justifications),
debate speeches (MPs explaining themselves in their own register), and
motioner with motivations. Standard valkompasses throw all of this away to
fit a 12-question quiz.

Three-axis design agreed for the project:
- *What* — position agreement (the standard signal)
- *Why* — value-frame agreement, from reasoning-text embeddings
- *How* — theory-of-change agreement (markets vs regulation, gradual vs
  radical) — vocabulary-detectable, deferred

User wants both an analytical artefact and a voter-facing tool eventually.
Shared backend, v0 = analytical, v0.5 = voter-facing.

## Data pipeline

`fetch_betankanden.py` pulls all 858 unique betänkanden from
`/utskottsforslag/<dok_id>` (XML). dok_id derives mechanically from
(rm, beteckning): HA01/HB01/HC01/HD01 + beteckning. No bulk dataset, but
1 req / 300ms over the API was fine — 4 minutes total, no 404s.

`parse_betankanden.py` extracts per-punkt: rubrik, forslag text,
motforslag party signature, reservation party-sets. Body text of
reservationer is in the bulky main document HTML (~MB of styling per
betänkande) — deferred. 8,143 punkter parsed; 2,480 of those link to a
voted chamber event (the rest were uncontested points passed without
roll-call).

99.2% of vote events have recommendation text. 1.5% don't because the
beteckning maps to a betänkande that wasn't found.

## Embedding model

`KBLab/sentence-bert-swedish-cased` — 768 dim, Swedish-specific from the
National Library's KB Lab. Smoke test: drivmedelsskatt sentences cluster
(sim 0.5), NATO is distant (sim 0.1). Fits all 2,480 recommendation
texts in 2 minutes on CPU.

## Topic clustering

KMeans on the (2480, 768) normalised embeddings with K=28.

Iteration 1: embedded rubrik + forslag text. Big "procedural" cluster
emerged (size 294) where the SBERT picked up the "Riksdagen avslår
motion..." formula across topics. Re-embedded using
`bet_titel + rubrik` (policy context, no procedural text). Iteration 2
gave clean policy clusters — sizes 28–161, mean 88.

The resulting 28 topics are coherent and committee-aligned:
labor market, climate & energy, schools, transport, taxes, child
welfare, foreign aid, NATO, migration, surveillance/crime, free speech,
housing, healthcare, culture, sports & substance abuse, etc.

Topic labels via TF-IDF on (rubrik + bet_titel), with year and number
tokens stripped via regex. Manual sanity-check passes.

## The position score caveat

```
stance_p,v = (n_Ja − n_Nej) / (n_Ja + n_Nej + n_Avstår)    ∈ [-1, +1]
```

By construction, **the cabinet (L, KD, M) scores +1.00 on every topic**
because cabinet members always vote Ja with the cabinet's proposal. The
score is not "supports more of X" — it's "agrees with the cabinet's
recommendation on X". That's structural, not ideological. The score has to
be read *relative* to the rest of the chamber.

Visible in `figures/11_compass_heatmap.png`: cabinet row is a solid red
band, SD a less-intense red, opposition rows in blue.

## The "why" axis — reasoning embeddings

Speeches link to betänkanden via `rel_dok_id` (83% of speeches do).
Per (party, topic) I picked up to 12 representative speeches (longest
non-replik chamber speeches by party MPs in the betänkanden of that
topic), truncated to 400 words to fit SBERT cleanly, embedded each,
then averaged → per-cell reasoning vector. 2,640 speeches embedded in 8
min on CPU. All 224 cells filled.

### Methodological gotchas (recorded so I don't repeat them)

- Case mismatch between speech `rel_dok_id` (e.g., `HA01JuU8`) and
  betänkande `dok_id` (same casing in XML). My initial join uppercased
  one side and not the other — got 345 speeches instead of 17k. Both
  sides must be normalised to the same case.
- Filter ordering matters. Word-count filter [80, 500] applied
  before joining dropped 75% of viable speeches because chamber
  debate speeches are longer than I assumed (median 850 words for
  the linked subset, not 250 as in the overall corpus). Lesson: filter
  on the joined population, not the source.
- SBERT cosine sims between Swedish political speeches all bunch around
  0.93–0.96 because they share the chamber's institutional vocabulary.
  Centering embeddings per topic (subtract topic mean) reveals
  party-specific reasoning differences that were hidden underneath.

## Findings

The headline artefact is the pair scatter
(`figures/15_pair_what_vs_why.png`): for each pair of parties, the
mean stance distance (WHAT, x-axis) vs the per-topic-centered
reasoning cosine (WHY, y-axis). Pairs above the trend line have more
*rhetorical* similarity than their vote alignment predicts; pairs
below have less.

**Marriages of convenience** (below the line — vote together but argue
apart):
- **S–C**: vote 91% together but rhetorically the most distant pair on
  the chart (−0.26). C votes with the opposition mostly but its
  argumentative DNA is markedly different from S's. Pure opposition-
  by-arithmetic.
- **L–SD**: vote 83% together but rhetorically distant (−0.21). The
  Tidö support pact looks much weaker in rhetoric than in votes.
- **MP–SD**: vote 67%, rhetoric −0.24.

**Genuine alignments** (above the line — vote AND argue together):
- **M–SD**: vote 83%, rhetoric +0.02 (one of only two positive cells).
  This is the M-rhetoric-bleeds-toward-SD signal from session 2 made
  more rigorous. The convergence is real on both axes — not just a vote
  coalition.
- **V–MP**: vote 93%, rhetoric +0.02. A coherent left.
- **MP–C**: rhetoric closer than votes — quiet "shared values" pair
  despite being on opposite sides of the bloc structure.

**Shared values, different policies** (above the line, low what but
relatively high why):
- **S–L** and **S–M**: across the bloc divide, these pairs sit higher on
  the rhetoric axis than their vote agreement would predict. The
  governance / establishment register survives the bloc split.

### What this tells us that the vote analysis didn't

The vote-based "Tidö collapse" finding (session 2) was that SD adopted
the cabinet's voting positions. The rhetorical analysis says: that
convergence is asymmetric across the cabinet. **M reflects SD; L and KD
don't.** When you build a voter-facing tool, "match to L because they
voted with SD" leaves a voter who hates SD's worldview confused. "Match
to M because they voted with SD AND argue like SD" is more honest.

### (B) Theory-of-change lexicon

Four small wordlist pairs over `reasoning_speeches.parquet`:
- market_vs_regulation
- prevention_vs_punishment
- state_vs_local
- universal_vs_targeted

Each speech scored on each dimension as
`(n_left − n_right) / (n_left + n_right) ∈ [-1, +1]`,
aggregated per (party, topic).

Caveats:
- Wordlists are loaded — I'm choosing what counts as "market" vs
  "regulation". They're explicit in the code so they can be revised.
- The vocabulary sides aren't frequency-balanced. "alla" and "stöd"
  are very common words and swamp the targeted/punishment sides,
  making most parties look universalist and prevention-oriented in
  absolute terms. *Relative* positions are still informative.

### Combined HOW similarity

`how_sim` per pair = mean of two rank-normalised components:
co-authorship cosine + lexicon mean-absolute-difference. See
`how_pair_sim.parquet` for the full table.

### Three-axis composite (figure 16_three_axis.png)

Each of the 28 party pairs gets a 3-D similarity vector (WHAT, WHY, HOW),
each rank-normalised to [0, 1]. Pair categories:

- **aligned on all three** (mean_n high, spread low): KD-M, L-M, L-KD,
  V-MP. These are coherent coalitions — vote, speech, mechanism all
  agree.
- **fragmented** (high cross-axis spread): the most interesting class.
  - **S-L** — highest spread of all (0.39). High WHY (rhetorical
    register similar) but low WHAT and HOW (oppositional votes,
    different mechanisms). Classic "shared establishment values,
    different politics".
  - **M-SD** — spread 0.37. High WHAT (vote 83%) and high WHY (+0.02
    centered cosine, one of only two positive pairs) but HOW drops to
    0.24. **M votes with SD and speaks like SD, but doesn't propose
    SD-style mechanisms.** Rhetorical alignment without theory-of-
    change alignment. This sharpens session 2's M→SD finding.
  - **S-C** — spread 0.34. Marriage of convenience: high WHAT (91%
    vote agreement) but low WHY and HOW. Vote-bloc partners with
    incompatible reasoning.
- **apart on all three**: V-L (lowest mean), V-KD, V-M, MP-L. Genuine
  cross-bloc opposites.
- **Surprise finding**: S-KD HOW similarity = 0.50 (5th highest of all

  surfaces in their mechanism vocabulary — they describe how to run a

- **Most consistent "opposite"**: V-SD has spread = 0.022 (lowest).
  They're equally distant on every axis — coherent enemies.

### What this gives the v0.5 voter-facing tool

The three axes can be presented to a voter separately. Sample matching
output for a hypothetical voter:

```
Your match by ...
  WHAT (policies)  : V        92% match
  WHY (values)     : S        84% match
  HOW (mechanisms) : C        78% match
```

This is more honest than "your closest party is X (87% match)" — it
surfaces the kind of disagreement the voter has with each party.

A voter whose values match the Greens but whose mechanism preferences
match the Centrists doesn't really fit either — and the standard
valkompass would never tell them that. The three-axis version does.

## (2) Reservation body text (the heavy unlock)

`src/fetch_reservations.py` pulls dokumentstatus XML (which wraps the
full HTML body) for every betänkande. 858 docs, 303 MB raw.

`src/parse_reservations.py` extracts reservation sections by
`<p class="Reservationsrubrik">` markers and walks forward until the
next heading or section break. **10,931 reservations parsed**, median
217 words, total 2.8M words of pure party-reasoning text.

### Coverage by signing pattern

85% are solo reservations. Per-party solo counts confirm the session 3
co-authorship finding (C tops at 2,279 solo, MP next at 2,012). Top
co-signing combinations: MP+V (465), MP+S+V (225), MP+S (195),
C+MP+S+V (133). Cabinet parties (L 9, KD 12, M 9) — barely write
reservations because they ARE the majority.

### Embedded reservation vectors

`src/embed_reservations.py`: per (party, topic), top 20 longest reservations
selected, SBERT-embedded, averaged. 2,688 reservations embedded in 8 min.
160 of 224 cells filled (cabinet parties have many empties — structural).

### How it sharpens WHY

`src/reservation_gap.py` re-computes the per-topic centred WHY cosine
from reservation vectors and compares against the speech-based one.

```
Pair    speech_why    reservation_why    delta
V-MP        +0.02           +0.27         +0.26     [confirmed + amplified]
V-S         -0.07           +0.19         +0.26     [left bloc unity is real]
S-MP        -0.13           +0.09         +0.22     [left bloc unity]
S-C         -0.26           +0.09         +0.34     [marriage-of-convenience overstated]
MP-C        -0.00           +0.06         +0.06
V-C         -0.05           -0.03         +0.02
```

**The session-3 "S–C is a marriage of convenience" finding overstated
the rhetorical gap.** Their *speeches* diverge — different MPs talk
about different things — but their *reservations* don't actively clash.
The speech-based view picked up topic-coverage differences as if they
were value-frame differences. Reservation text is the cleaner signal.

**V-MP went from +0.02 to +0.27** — a much stronger reasoning alignment
than chamber speeches revealed.

**Cabinet vs opposition lands at −0.79 to −0.89** — clean polarisation
visible in the reservation matrix in a way it wasn't in speeches.

Figure: `figures/18_why_speech_vs_reservation.png` shows the three
matrices side by side (speech / reservation / combined).

The reservation-based vectors also become the natural content for the
voter-facing blind-argument tool — they're already in the right format
("vi anser att förslaget under punkt N borde ha följande lydelse…").

## (3) Temporal HOW

`src/temporal_how.py`.

### Lexicon drift per party (2025/26 − 2022/23)

```
                          market  prevention   state  univ
                          v reg   v punish     v loc  v targ
V                          -0.04   -0.03       +0.03  +0.03
S                          -0.03   -0.08       +0.23  -0.01
MP                         +0.18   -0.28       -0.11  -0.04
C                           0.00   -0.30       -0.21  -0.02
L                          -0.11   +0.08       +0.06  -0.03
KD                         +0.04   -0.01       +0.02  -0.02
M                          +0.16   +0.03       -0.11  +0.02
SD                         +0.06   +0.02       +0.05  -0.02
```

Findings:
- **C and MP shift toward punishment vocabulary** (−0.30, −0.28).
  The discourse-shift on crime that has dominated Swedish politics
  since 2022 reaches across the bloc. C in particular abandons its
  prevention-side starting position.
- **M moves toward market vocabulary** (+0.16). The cabinet's economic
  rightward shift shows up linguistically in M but not in KD/L.
- **MP also moves toward market** (+0.18). Surprising — could be
  procurement / industrial-policy adaptation under the new EU framework.
  Worth a follow-up.
- **S moves toward state vocabulary** (+0.23). Reaffirmation of
  traditional SAP messaging.
- **C moves toward local vocabulary** (−0.21). Identity-reinforcement
  vs the cabinet's centralism.
- **L moves toward regulation vocabulary** (−0.11) AND prevention
  (+0.08). L is rhetorically the most distinct cabinet party in 2025/26.
- universal_vs_targeted is stable across the board.

### SD's reservation collapse — the headline temporal finding

```
SD reservations per riksmöte:
  2022/23: 749
  2023/24: 402
  2024/25: 574
  2025/26:  17    ← 97% drop
```

Session 2's vote-agreement finding (SD↔cabinet 73% → 98%) showed that
SD started voting with the cabinet in the election year. **This shows
SD also stopped writing its own alternatives.** By 2025/26 SD has
not just voted with the cabinet — it has stopped putting its own
positions on the formal record. Full behavioural fusion.

Opposition counts went the other way:
- V 533 → 851 (+60%)
- MP 658 → 936 (+42%)
- C 949 → 701 (−26%)
- S 481 → 813 (+69%)

Opposition is more active because they have more to dissent against —
a cabinet+SD bloc that votes unanimously.

## What's still missing

- Reservation excerpts haven't been deduplicated for the voter tool —
  many reservations recycle paragraphs across betänkanden.
- For the cabinet's WHY signal we still rely on speech vectors. A
  better cabinet signal would come from the *betänkande motivering*
  (majority opinion text) — already in the cached HTML, not parsed.
- The lexicon wordlists have known frequency imbalances. A future
  iteration should normalise per-side word frequency or use IDF
  weighting against the chamber corpus.
