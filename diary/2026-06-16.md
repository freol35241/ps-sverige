# 2026-06-16

## 2026 manifesto scoping

Three months out from the September 2026 election, ran an inventory of
which parties have published 2026 election material:

- **Liberalerna**: full valmanifest "För din frihet", 40 pages, 9,452
  words, presented 2 June 2026 by Simona Mohamsson. Comparable in scope
  to the 2022 set.
  https://www.liberalerna.se/wp-content/uploads/liberalernas-valmanifest-2026-40s-komprimerad.pdf
- **Vänsterpartiet**: preliminary congress valplatform, ~4 pages.
- **Socialdemokraterna**: "Plan för Sverige" three-pillar platform from
  February 2026; not a full manifesto.
- **Centerpartiet**: short valplan (Jan 2026), 3 priorities.
- **Miljöpartiet**: 2026 valmanifest exists as a password-protected
  draft.
- **Moderaterna**: "Handlingsprogram för 2030-talet" feeds into the
  manifesto, not the manifesto itself.
- **Kristdemokraterna**: no 2026 manifesto; recent material is a
  delivery-on-2022-pledges progress report.
- **Sverigedemokraterna**: no 2026 valplattform; only the September
  2025 "Inriktning" signalling document.

Decision: hold the full eight-party three-layer analysis until early
September when most parties are expected to finalise. Run the
single-party analysis for L now since it is the only complete document
and L is also the analytically charged case from the main article
(the L-defectors finding).

## Findings for L

- Manifesto grew 28 % (7,375 → 9,452 words; 469 → 673 substantive
  sentences). Element shares essentially unchanged: diagnosis 6.0 →
  4.0 %, end-state 10.4 → 10.8 %, mechanism 11.1 → 9.7 %.
- Value vocabulary moved more. **frihet** more than doubled (2.4 →
  6.2 per 1k words), matching the manifesto title. **trygghet**,
  **tillväxt** and **ansvar** all rose. **klimat** fell 4.0 → 2.3
  and **demokrati** fell 2.7 → 1.0. Shift toward classical-liberal
  core reinforced by KD- and M-coded values.
- nationell_identitet fell 15.6 → 11.2 but this metric mostly counts
  occurrences of "Sverige"/"svensk", so part of the drop is just the
  longer document being more discursive per "Sverige".
- Topic-matched gap vs L's chamber: 0.76 (n=9) → 0.69 (n=13). Looks
  like L drifted away from its own chamber work.
- But the cross-party comparison shows the drop is uniform: L 2026 vs
  every party's chamber reasoning is ~0.07 lower than L 2022. The
  2026 manifesto is in a more public-facing register, not closer to
  any particular party. The life-journey framing of "För din frihet"
  (eight life-stage sections) does not map cleanly to parliamentary
  committee work, of any party.
- In both 2022 and 2026, L's lead over M as the closest match is
  small (0.76 vs 0.76 in 2022; 0.69 vs 0.68 in 2026). The L caucus
  writes its election material in a register that does not strongly
  distinguish itself from its cabinet partners. This held across the
  mandate and is not a 2026 development. The L-defectors gap is
  therefore between manifesto register and floor-vote behaviour, not
  between L's manifesto and M's committee work.

## Two-mandate pipeline build (autonomous session, evening)

The single-day push into the longitudinal analysis. Started with the
2018 manifestos (already in the SND archive, no scraping needed) and
went through to the full per-mandate analytical infrastructure.

### Decisions taken

- **K=28 stays.** A sweep across K in {16, 20, 24, 28, 32, 36} on the
  union of 2018-22 + 2022-26 decision-point embeddings found silhouette
  essentially flat from K=24 to K=36, with K=32 fractionally above
  K=28 (0.0004) but cluster sizes dropping below 50. K=28 keeps
  minimum cluster size 81 and coherent committee-aligned labels.
  Documented in k_selection_scores.csv.
- **Unified KMeans on the union.** Same 28 topics across both
  mandates. Stability check vs the original single-mandate clustering
  shows mean majority share 0.79, with 18 of 28 old topics landing
  ≥80 % in a single new topic. Five topics substantially restructured.
- **L=28 longitudinal stability.** All 28 unified topics have between
  0.36 and 0.69 share_18_22, no mandate-specific topic.

### Compute artefacts produced

- 2018-22 corpus: votes_long_18_22, vote_events_18_22,
  vote_event_texts_18_22, embed_recommendations_18_22, mps_18_22,
  votes_matrix_18_22, reservations_18_22.
- Unified topics: topics_unified, topic_meta_unified, topic_stability.

- Per-mandate PCA, defectors, per-topic stance, agreement trends.
- Cross-mandate pair vote correlations.
- Manifesto register trajectories per party 2018→2022 (+L 2026).

### Headline longitudinal findings

- **The Tidö bloc is a 2022-26 creation, not a pre-existing
  structure.** M+KD+L+SD unanimity at 16-30 % across 2018-22, jumps
  to 67 % at the start of 2022-26 and climbs to 98 % by the election
  year (figure 51).
- **The "L defectors" finding from the main article does not exist
  in 2018-22.** Top defectors there were V members (Gudrun Nordborg,
  Jonas Sjöstedt) and S members voting like MP. In 2022-26 the top
  list is dominated by L members voting like KD or M. The L-as-
  fifth-cluster phenomenon emerged with Tidö (figure 52).
- **Manifesto register is not a stable party identity.** Large
  shifts 2018→2022 for SD (diagnosis 5.7→12.8 %), KD (diag 3.9→10.9,
  end 9.9→3.1 %), C (diag 9.6→4.7 %), M (mech 8.2→20.5 %). V and L
  are stable across cycles (figure 50).
- **L-M and L-KD vote correlation snapped from ~0.1 to 0.99+ at the
  mandate boundary.** Pearson on per-year mean vote vectors. The
  pair-level convergence is sharp and dated to the Tidö formation,
  not gradual (figure 55).
- **SD vote-vs-reasoning shifts.** Under unified topic IDs, M-SD's
  reasoning similarity is -0.56 (well below the cross-pair trend),
  not above as the published main article currently claims. SD votes
  with the cabinet but writes in opposition register. The main
  article's "M's reasoning register having shifted toward SD's"
  claim needs revision when the article is restructured.
- **The reservation collapse is a longer arc than the article
  showed.** SD's full 8-year reservation trajectory: 853, 697, 990,
  1093 (2018-22 opposition years) → 749, 402, 574, 17 (2022-26 Tidö
  years). The election-year crash to 17 is sharp, but the gradual
  decline through 2022-26 is also part of the story (figure 57).
- **M, KD, L's transition to cabinet** is visible in the same figure:
  reservations dropped from ~270-910 per year in 2018-22 to 0-5 per
  year throughout 2022-26.
